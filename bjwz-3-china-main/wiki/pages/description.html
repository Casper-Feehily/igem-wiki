Background and Inspiration

     Aflatoxins (AFTs, 312 ~ 346 kD) are a class of colorless and odorless secondary metabolites produced by a variety of fungi such as Aspergillus flavus. They are highly toxic and can induce carcinogenic, toxic and malformation effects. In addition, due to the wide distribution of the toxin, aflatoxins will inevitably be directly or indirectly touched by humans during contact and ingestion with contaminated agricultural products and animal products derived from the use of contaminated feed, resulting in toxicity. In 1993, aflatoxins were listed as Grade I strong carcinogens by the World Health Organization Cancer Institute, and there are more than 20 aflatoxins that have been found and determined in structure, of which aflatoxins B1(AFB1) is the most carcinogenic. Studies have found that long-term low-dose intake of AFB1 by humans can lead to embryo deformities, mutations, and primary cancers, resulting in immune suppression and gene mutation of organisms, while one-time large intake can cause acute poisoning or even death, and AFB1 can also remain in animal products to further endanger human health. In addition, aflatoxins have a high detection rate in cereals, dairy products, fats, spices and fruits, so aflatoxins have caused serious negative effects on people's normal social production and life.

Product Design

    To address this, the BJWZ-3-China team is using synthetic biology methods to modify genetically engineered probiotics, aiming to develop a probiotic additive that can degrade aflatoxins in dairy products, in order to reduce the losses caused by aflatoxin contamination from various perspectives.
      We have chosen a probiotic commonly used in dairy fermentation - Lactobacillus rhamnosus as our chassis. As a Gram-positive bacterium, its peptidoglycan layer can physically adsorb up to 80% of aflatoxins in the environment, serving an enrichment function.
      However, this binding is not stable, and after a period of time, aflatoxins can revert to their free state. Therefore, we have introduced the gene for aflatoxin detoxifying enzyme into this strain, giving it the ability to completely degrade aflatoxins. Aflatoxin detoxifying enzyme (ADTZ) is a luminescent enzyme found in porous fungi in the Pleurotaceae family, possessing the efficient capability to detoxify aflatoxins by disrupting the C8, 9 double bond in the furofuran ring to render them non-toxic.

Finely-tuned Control Device

      We have also engineered a finely-tuned control device so that the engineered bacteria only secrete the aflatoxin detoxifying enzyme when aflatoxins are detected in the environment. Indiscriminate secretion of the enzyme could burden the engineered strain excessively, disrupt the normal surrounding microenvironment, and even impact human intestinal health. We have utilized nanobodies as tools.
     Nanobodies, naturally lacking light chains found in the peripheral blood of alpacas, consist of only one variable heavy chain (VHH) and two conventional CH2 and CH3 regions, unlike single-chain antibody fragments (scFv) that are prone to mutual adhesion and aggregation brought about by artificial modifications.The cloned VHH structure expressed individually exhibits comparable structural stability to the original heavy chain antibody as well as binding activity to the antigen, making it the smallest unit known to bind the target antigen. We will use a nanoantibody, Nb26, which can specifically recognize and bind aflatoxin.
  This nanoantibody will be combined with a nano-luciferase enzyme (Addgene_87696) and delivered separately into the product. Upon binding to aflatoxin, the nano-luciferase enzyme will be activated, producing fluorescence at a specific wavelength. Additionally, we are expressing a photosensitive channel protein on the cell membrane of engineered bacteria that will change conformation upon receiving the specific wavelength of fluorescence, opening the channel to allow entry of signal molecules present in the environment. Subsequently, through relevant promoters, the detoxifying enzyme for aflatoxin will be initiated for expression.

Suicide Switch

    Furthermore, to prevent potential contamination from the engineered strain escaping into the environment, a glucose-controlled system has been implemented as a suicide switch. Considering the differences in glucose concentrations in the human intestinal tract and dairy environments compared to the external environment, the PT-acrp promoter responsive to changes in glucose concentration has been chosen. To ensure no bacterial contents escape upon cell death and to mitigate any risks, the mazF gene is being utilized. This gene encodes an endoribonuclease that cleaves RNA at ACA sites, leading to microbial death, thus facilitating cell suicide without bacterial cell lysis. The PT-acr promoter controls the expression of the suicide gene mazF, which activates under low glucose concentrations to achieve the desired suicidal effect.

Innovation and Application

    The innovation of this project lies in the first-time combination of physical adsorption with degradation of aflatoxin, along with the design of a unique fine-tuning device. This product can be used for the degradation of aflatoxin in dairy products. Additionally, Lactobacillus rhamnosus itself is a common probiotic, so after human intake of related products, it can temporarily colonize the human intestine, degrade small amounts of aflatoxin ingested by humans, and protect humans from aflatoxin harm in a short period of time.
